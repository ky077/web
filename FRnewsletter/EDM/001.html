Using VR technology to create an immersive language learning experience
“We hope that our virtual reality and mobile learning will work not only for Mandarin learners, but also for English learners.” Dr. Yu-Ju Lan said in a research exhibition. She led the team in developing a 3D environment that supports language learning for children aged 9 and above as well as for adults. At the same time, she cares about students with special educational needs and constructs an immersive learning scene to assist autistic youth in language learning.

NTNU’s language technology is integrated into teaching, making a solid debut
eMPOWER is a Mandarin e-learning platform covering listening, speaking, reading, and writing skills. The platform allows teachers to create teaching materials, offer courses, track student progress, and administer exams. Currently, the users of eMPOWER have spanned across more than 15 countries in Asia, the United States and Europe.
