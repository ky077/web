Research News
Epigenetic Regulation of Neuronal Pathways as
Biomarkers to Explore Cognitive Function
in Adolescents

An NTNU research team led by Dr. Li-Ching Lee has identified epigenetic biomarkers—particularly non-coding microRNAs (miRNAs)—that may predict how adolescents learn, remember, and reason. Their findings reveal key pathways, including the CaMKIIα/SIRT1/BDNF system, that link blood plasma signals to academic performance on major learning assessments.

Most notably, the study suggests that specific miRNAs could serve as non-invasive biomarkers of cognition, offering new tools to understand and support student learning. By bridging molecular biology and education, Dr. Lee’s team is positioning NTNU at the global forefront of neuroscience and learning sciences.

Research News
Unique Brain Connectivity in Gelotophobes
on the Fear of Laughter

Why do some people fear laughter? A new NTNU study led by Prof. Hsueh-Chih Chen reveals the neural basis of gelotophobia—the fear of being laughed at. Using resting-state fMRI scans of 183 participants, researchers found that gelotophobes show:

  • Stronger amygdala–striatum connectivity, linked to heightened fear responses.
  • More self-focused processing, interpreting laughter as ridicule.
  • Hyper-attention to social cues, fueling anxiety in social settings.

By contrast, non-gelotophobes demonstrated more adaptive brain connectivity, supporting flexible emotional regulation. These findings suggest gelotophobia has measurable neurobiological underpinnings, paving the way for new insights into social anxiety and humor psychology.

Research News
It’s not about sense of humor, but your emotional state!

NTNU researcher Prof. Hsueh-Chih Chen and his team found that humor appreciation depends not just on jokes, but on our emotional state. In a study with 96 participants:

  • Positive moods boosted amusement ratings across joke types.
  • Negative moods reduced enjoyment of nonsense jokes, requiring greater playfulness to be appreciated.
  • Incongruity-resolution jokes stayed funny regardless of mood, proving more robust across emotional states.

These findings suggest tailoring humor to mood can enhance therapy, teaching, and daily communication—showing that how we feel shapes how we laugh.

Engagement News
NTNU and New Taipei City Launch AISI:
Pioneering AI Self-Learning for Smart Education

On April 9, 2025, NTNU and the New Taipei City Education Department launched AISI (https://aisi.tw), a next-generation AI self-learning platform. Piloted across New Taipei schools, AISI supports students from elementary to high school with personalized guidance while helping teachers design lessons more efficiently.

Early feedback is promising—teachers report higher efficiency and engagement, while students describe AISI as a “smart learning partner.” By adding AI-assisted learning to New Taipei’s Four Learning Modes, this initiative sets a new milestone for smart education in Taiwan.

Engagement News
NTNU × Xinsheng Elementary School Launch
“AI Robot Teacher × Bilingual STEAM Summer Camp”

From August 11–15, 2025, NTNU’s IRELS partnered with Xinsheng Elementary School to host the AI Robot Teacher × Bilingual STEAM Summer Camp. Over five immersive days, students engaged in bilingual, hands-on learning, interacting with AI-powered teaching robots, tackling gamified challenges, and building their own “Little Octopus Vacuum Cleaner” prototypes.

The camp followed the 6E Learning Model, blending AI, English, and STEAM subjects to strengthen creativity, problem-solving, and teamwork. With enthusiastic feedback from students, parents, and educators, this initiative showcases how AI-assisted, story-driven pedagogy can inspire joyful and future-ready learning.

Six IRELS Scholars Honored
with 2024 NSTC Academic Research Awards

Six scholars from NTNU’s Institute for Research Excellence in Learning Sciences (IRELS) received top recognition in the 2024 Academic Research Awards by Taiwan’s National Science and Technology Council (NSTC), spanning three categories:

  • Distinguished Research Fellows: Prof. Yao-Ting Sung, Prof. Ying-Shao Hsu

This honor underscores NTNU’s leadership in advancing education, learning sciences, and interdisciplinary research on the global stage.
