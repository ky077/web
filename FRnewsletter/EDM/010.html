ITSC Introduction
The International Taiwan Studies Center at NTNU: Making Taiwan Studies Global

Since its establishment, ITSC has served as a foundation for promoting Taiwan-focused research and building international academic networks. Backed by NTNU’s strengths in Taiwan-related research, Mandarin education, and EMI development, the Center has expanded its role as a global hub for Taiwan Studies. In 2025, ITSC supported a 32-member scholarly team and continued to foster enduring international partnerships through conferences, joint research initiatives, and public-facing knowledge dissemination.

Cover Story
Director Nikky Lin: On the Necessity of Establishing the International Taiwan Studies Center

In this issue’s cover story, Director Nikky Lin reflects on why an international Taiwan Studies center is both timely and necessary. As Taiwan gains increasing global attention through its geopolitical significance, technological leadership, and historical complexity, there is a growing need for an institution capable of connecting domestic scholarship with overseas Taiwan Studies centers. ITSC addresses that need by building stable international networks, integrating research resources across NTNU, and helping Taiwan-based perspectives make meaningful contributions to global theory and academic discourse.

Research News
Distinguished Professor Fang Wei-ta: Going International with Local Environmental Knowledge

Professor Fang Wei-ta’s recent work demonstrates how Taiwan’s local environmental knowledge can speak powerfully to global sustainability debates. His 2025 research includes a significant international publication on Indigenous ecological knowledge and green ethics, as well as work on wetlands, blue carbon, and environmental governance. Together, these projects position Taiwan not merely as a case study, but as a source of theoretical insight and practical models for international environmental humanities and sustainability research.

Research News
Professor Lu Yu-hsiu:
Hearing Indigenous Music of the 1960s

Professor Lu Yu-hsiu’s research addresses a crucial challenge in Indigenous music studies: how to preserve not only documentation, but sound itself as a living carrier of memory and knowledge. In 2025, she published two albums based on rare recordings collected during Taiwan’s First Folk Song Collection Movement in the late 1960s. These restored works,

presented in both Chinese and English, help revive historically significant musical archives while contributing to intergenerational transmission and broader international understanding of Taiwan’s Indigenous cultures.

Research News
Professor Khoo Hui-lu:
Strengthening the Subjectivity of Taiwanese Taigi

Professor Khoo Hui-lu continues to advance the international visibility and academic status of Taiwanese Taigi through research in phonetics, sociolinguistics, and language policy. A major milestone this year was the publication of The Phonetics of Taiwanese by Cambridge University Press, a rare English-language academic monograph devoted specifically to Taiwanese. Her recent work also examines public attitudes toward language diversity in Taiwan, including accents associated with Taiwanese, Mandarin, Hakka, and Southeast Asian linguistic communities.

Research News
Professor Craig Smith:
Exploring Inter-Asian Intellectual History

Professor Craig Smith’s work brings translation studies into dialogue with intellectual history by examining how translators and interpreters shaped ideas, identities, and regional imaginaries across East Asia. Building on his broader work on Chinese Asianism, his current Taiwan

-focused research investigates the historical agency of translators working among East Asian languages. Taiwan’s multilingual history makes it a particularly rich site for understanding how translation has mediated power, ambiguity, and cultural exchange in the making of modern East Asia.

Engagement News
The 5th World Congress of Taiwan Studies

Held on May 21–23, 2025 and co-organized by Academia Sinica and ITSC, the Fifth World Congress of Taiwan Studies was the largest Taiwan Studies-focused event to date. Featuring 155 panels, over 400 paper presentations, and more than 750 participants from 30 countries, the congress demonstrated both the maturity and the remarkable interdisciplinarity of the field. Beyond the scale of the event, it created valuable space for dialogue among scholars, institutions, and policy stakeholders, further strengthening Taiwan Studies as a global scholarly network.

Overseas Events

Throughout 2025, ITSC continued to deepen its collaborations with partner institutions abroad, including UCLA, UCSB, INALCO, the University of Vienna, and the University of Oxford. These exchanges extended Taiwan Studies into international academic and public arenas through jointly organized conferences and symposia. Featured events included the UCLA conference on material and technological cultures in Taiwan and a Paris symposium on the transformation of Taiwanese literature across translation, adaptation, and interpretation. Together, these initiatives broadened the global reach of Taiwan-related scholarship and cultural discourse.

Other Outreach

ITSC’s public engagement extends beyond conferences and publications. Through its podcast, newsletter, and multilingual digital initiatives, the Center continues to make Taiwan Studies more accessible to broader audiences. The NTNU International Taiwan Studies Center Podcast introduces global scholars and their research to listeners around the world, while the Center’s newsletter shares developments in the field on a regular basis. ITSC also maintains TaiGiddy, an innovative online platform for Taiwanese-language learning with English and Japanese interfaces, designed to foster cultural connection through accessible and engaging digital learning.

Upcoming Events
Transculturation: The 26th Annual International Conference of the Cultural Studies Association

Co-organized by ITSC and hosted at NTNU’s Heping Campus, this conference explores how culture moves across and through language, sound, text, image, history, technology, and media. Organized around the concepts of “trans” and “culturalization,” the event invites participants to consider transformation, hybridity, transition, and regeneration across a wide range of topics. The conference reflects ITSC’s continuing interest in cross-disciplinary dialogue and in situating Taiwan within larger cultural and theoretical conversations.

Upcoming Events
Keywords in Taiwan Theory III Workshop

Scheduled for September 19–20, 2026, this workshop continues a long-term effort to link theory and Taiwan through the “keyword method.” Building on the earlier Keywords of Taiwan Theory volumes, the workshop will introduce new concepts while also revisiting influential older keywords in Taiwan’s humanities and social sciences. By exploring continuity, revision, and reinterpretation, the event seeks to deepen scholarly dialogue on how Taiwan’s historical and intellectual experience can continue to generate new theoretical insights.

Awards & Honors
Work by Chen Yu-jen on Taiwan’s Culinary History Honored with Award in Japan

Professor Chen Yu-jen’s book on the history of Taiwan cuisine has received the 16th Shizuo Tsuji Gastronomy Award in Japan, an important recognition of Taiwan-related scholarship in the international culinary field. Her work traces how changes in politics, identity, and concepts of nationhood have shaped Taiwan’s culinary culture over the past century. The award is especially notable because she is the first foreign researcher to receive it, and the book is also the first translated work to be honored.
